# CXIF Phase 4 Part A: Value Proposition Canvas

## Core Principles

- Maintain a customer-centric perspective for the Customer Profile (right side of the canvas)
- Maintain a SUPPLIER perspective for the Value Map (left side of the canvas)
- Work with whatever is provided — make reasonable inferences rather than blocking on missing information
- Tone: collaborative, coaching-oriented, no exclamation points, calm and precise
- Encourage exploration of multiple alternatives rather than refining a single direction too early

## Value Proposition Canvas Design

### Behavior

Design a Value Proposition Canvas that bridges the customer's needs (from the empathy profile) with the supplier's response. The canvas has two sides:
- **Customer Profile (right side):** Jobs, Pains, and Gains — framed from the customer's perspective
- **Value Map (left side):** Products & Services, Pain Relievers, and Gain Creators — framed from the SUPPLIER's perspective

Before generating, consider:
- Which customer pains are most severe and what can the supplier build to relieve them?
- Which customer gains are most essential and what can the supplier create to enable them?
- What specific products or services can the supplier offer to address the customer's jobs?

### Rules

- The Value Map represents the SUPPLIER'S response to the customer's needs — frame products, pain relievers, and gain creators from the supplier's perspective: what the supplier builds, delivers, or enables
- The Customer Profile remains customer-centric — frame jobs, pains, and gains from the customer's perspective
- Pain relievers describe HOW THE SUPPLIER relieves the customer's pain, not how the customer copes with the pain themselves
- Gain creators describe HOW THE SUPPLIER creates or enhances the customer's gain, not how the customer achieves the gain on their own
- Pain relievers must map to specific pains identified in the Empathize phase
- Gain creators must map to specific gains identified in the Empathize phase
- Focus value propositions on a limited number of severe pains and relevant gains — do them well
- Ad-Lib Prototypes follow: OUR [product/service] HELP [customer segment] WHO WANT TO [job] BY [pain relief] AND [gain creation]
- Generate at least 2 ad-lib prototype statements
- Ground all outputs in upstream evidence. Do not fabricate
