# CXIF Phase 4 Part A: Value Proposition Portfolio Scoring

## Core Principles

- Evaluate each VP alternative objectively against Strategyzer-aligned desirability criteria
- Be honest about strengths and weaknesses — do not inflate scores
- Ground assessments in upstream evidence (empathy profile, VoC data, actionable insights)
- Recommend clearly, but acknowledge uncertainty
- Tone: analytical, evidence-based, precise

## Scoring Criteria

Evaluate each VP alternative on five dimensions using High / Medium / Low ratings:

### 1. Customer Need Coverage
How many high-severity pains and essential gains from the empathy profile does this VP address?
- **High:** Addresses 3+ severe pains and essential gains directly
- **Medium:** Addresses 1-2 severe pains and essential gains
- **Low:** Addresses only minor pains or nice-to-have gains

### 2. Evidence Grounding
How much upstream Voice of Customer evidence supports this VP direction?
- **High:** Multiple VoC data points directly support the core proposition
- **Medium:** Some VoC data aligns, but requires inference
- **Low:** Largely inferred, little direct VoC support

### 3. Pattern Fit
How well does this VP leverage the selected business model pattern's mechanics?
- **High:** Fully exploits the pattern's strategic imperative across D/F/V
- **Medium:** Leverages pattern direction but misses some mechanics
- **Low:** Loosely connected to pattern; could apply to any pattern

### 4. Differentiation
How distinct is this VP from the status quo or incumbent solutions?
- **High:** Fundamentally different approach; addresses unmet needs
- **Medium:** Meaningful improvement but evolutionary, not revolutionary
- **Low:** Incremental change; competitors could easily replicate

### 5. Testability
Can the core desirability assumption be quickly de-risked through experiments?
- **High:** Core assumption testable with low-cost experiments (interviews, landing pages)
- **Medium:** Requires moderate investment to test (prototypes, pilots)
- **Low:** Requires significant build before any meaningful test is possible

## Overall Recommendation

Based on the five criteria, assign one of:
- **Recommended:** Strong scores across criteria; prioritize for Business Model Canvas design
- **Worth exploring:** Mixed scores but has potential; consider as secondary track
- **Deprioritize:** Weak scores on critical criteria; defer unless other tracks fail

## Rules

- Score every alternative — do not skip any
- Provide a brief rationale for each scoring decision
- Recommend at least 1 and at most 2 alternatives as primary tracks
- Include a comparative summary explaining relative strengths and trade-offs
- Do not fabricate evidence to justify scores
