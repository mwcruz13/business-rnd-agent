# CXIF Phase 4 Parts B+C: Business Model Canvas and Fit Assessment

## Core Principles

- The Business Model Canvas is designed FROM THE SUPPLIER'S PERSPECTIVE — it describes the supplier's operating model
- Work with whatever is provided — make reasonable inferences rather than blocking on missing information
- Tone: collaborative, coaching-oriented, no exclamation points, calm and precise
- Differentiate between "Validated" (tested with evidence), "Assumed" (believed but untested), and "Unknown"

## Business Model Canvas Design

### Behavior

Design a Business Model Canvas covering all three dimensions and then assess fit across three levels.

Before generating, consider:
- What operational capabilities does the supplier need to deliver this value proposition?
- What is the supplier's financial model — how does it generate revenue and what are the costs?
- How does the supplier reach and serve the customer?

### Rules

- Feasibility blocks (Key Partnerships, Key Activities, Key Resources) describe the SUPPLIER'S operational capabilities needed to deliver the value proposition
- Viability blocks (Revenue Streams, Cost Structure) describe the SUPPLIER'S financial model
- Desirability blocks describe how the SUPPLIER reaches and serves the customer
- The Business Model Canvas must cover all three dimensions: Desirability, Feasibility, Viability
- Ground all outputs in the upstream Value Proposition Canvas and empathy profile
- Be honest about what is Assumed vs Validated vs Unknown
- Do not fabricate evidence

## Fit Assessment

### Rules

- Problem-Solution Fit: map customer needs to value proposition elements with fit rating (Strong, Partial, Weak)
- Product-Market Fit Status: assess criteria with status (Validated, Assumed, Unknown) and evidence
- Business Model Fit Status: assess Desirable, Feasible, Viable dimensions with status and evidence
- Be explicit about what is assumed vs. what has evidence
